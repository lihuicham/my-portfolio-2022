Know Me More

Facts About Me

Academics

What courses do I study ?

I am a Year 2 undergraduate in National University of Singapore (NUS) - School of Computing, majoring in Business Analytics (Honours). I do two minors, namely Interactive Media Development and Statistics. I am also an ASEAN scholar in NUS.

A Data Storyteller

How do I wish to contribute ?

I am particulary interested in data storytelling. In today's society where data is the new gold, I wish to combine my knowledge in data analytics and frontend development to create impactful stories on social issues and discover business insights.

Nobody wants to see rows of numbers in an Excel sheet nor unfamiliar statistics formula in a report, most people want beautiful visualizations that spill the facts in one glance. As such, it is my responsibility to turn boring numbers and strange data into meaningful insights.

Student Life

How do I spend my time outside school ?

As an international student, I stay in a hostel. Living together with brilliant minds, we work hard, but play hard too. Not only that I am the Sports Director of the college, I am also the Head of Basketball and Netball Interest Groups. I look forward to basketball sessions every weekend and have forged good friendship with my teammates after ICG 2021 where we won Silver in Basketball (F) category.

Aside from sports, I love music too. After passing ABRSM Grade 8 in Piano, I realized that music is more than a subject that is examinable. It is a lifestyle. Now, I play piano accompaniments for pop songs and sing along with the melody. I have a lot of fun from this !

A Polyglot

How many languages can I speak ?

As a Malaysian who went through 12 years of education in Malaysia, I can understand English, Mandarin and Malay. I am most fluent in English and Chinese as I use both of them on a daily basis. With an advanced Malay level, I can read, write and speak Malay too.

Furthermore, I can understand and speak Cantonese and Korean. Cantonese is widely spoken in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia among business professionals and fluency in the language allows me to communicate with my past colleagues easily. The global influence of K-pop, Korean dramas and variety shows prompted me to pick up Korean over my gap year, and since then, I achieve an intermediate proficiency in Korean.

Struggles and Hardships

What's my attitude towards hardships ?

I came to Singapore to pursue my tertiary education in NUS. Unlike the past where I was only a traveller in Singapore, I am now a student here. The pace of study and life in Singapore is surely more intensed than what I used to experience in Malaysia. Due to the huge gap in academic knowledge compared to the local students and zero experience in programming, I struggled to complete the assignments and could not ace in my exams. However, I was not defeated by these hardships, they inspired and motivated me to work harder than my peers.

My proactiveness in asking questions guided my learning process and I manage to teach others too. Although the GPA is an important indicator of a student's ability, I did not limit myself to only take subjects that I am already comfortable in the areas of knowledge taught. I am always looking for areas of improvement, challenged myself to learn new skills and put those skills into action by doing personal projects.

Gap Year

Did I regret taking gap year ?

In short, no.

I took a gap year in Jun 2019 - Jun 2020. I was uncertained of my decision of taking a year off after A-levels. However, I found myself and seize the chance to be who I truly am in the gap year. Despite my excellent result in A-levels Biology and Chemistry, I figured that my personality is not suitable for a career in the medical or healthcare field. Hence, I sought for other options. I discovered my passion in analytics and aspired to turn data into meaningful insights. After serious consideration, I applied to NUS Business Analytics. All of these would not have happened if I did not allow myself some time to think and recharge.

I worked as a private tutor during the gap year and my earnings was able to cover my daily expenses and be independent of my family's financial support.
